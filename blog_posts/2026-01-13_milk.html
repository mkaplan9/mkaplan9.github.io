Warming breastmilk

2026-01-13

Our son eats breastmilk straight from the fridge. At various points I've wondered if he still prefers it at room temperature, but if so the difference is small. But, I started wondering if we should still warm up his breastmilk to get him more calories. He's a pretty good eater, but I always worry that he's getting enough. This week I finally got curious enough to calculate the caloric difference between milk from the fridge (at about 38°F) vs. at room temperature (at about 68°F). Simplifying body temperature as 98°F, we have

  • A ΔT of 30°F vs. 60°F
  • A specific heat of breastmilk of about 0.0166 kcal / 1 oz / degree F
  • 1 oz of breastmilk has about 20 kcal of energy
  • He eats about 30 oz a day

$$ (\frac{1\ kcal}{1\ oz}\ for\ 60°F\ change\ -\ \frac{0.5\ kcal}{1\ oz}\ for\ 30°F\ change)\ *\ \frac{1\ oz\ breastmilk}{20\ kcal}\ *\ \frac{30\ oz}{1\ day}
=\ \frac{1.5\ oz}{1\ day}\ -\ \frac{0.75\ oz}{1\ day}
=\ 0.75\ oz/day $$

0.75 oz/day is less than I was expecting! That comes out to about 2.5% fewer calories if we don't warm the milk, which doesn't seem worth worrying about to me.
